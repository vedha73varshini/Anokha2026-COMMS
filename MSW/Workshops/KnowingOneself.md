# Knowing Oneself – Way to Student's Well-Being

This workshop is a reflective, interactive session designed to help students understand their emotions, values, strengths, and thinking patterns for better mental well-being. It promotes emotional resilience, mindfulness, healthy coping strategies, and positive self-image through guided activities and discussions.

## Speaker

- **Name**: Dr. Aravind Raj E
- **Company: Name of the company**: NIMHANS, Bengaluru
- Dr. Aravind Raj E is an Additional Professor in Psychiatric Social Work at NIMHANS, specializing in disaster response, migrant workers’ mental health, and community mental health; he has led numerous national-level training and capacity-building programs, delivering evidence-based, community-focused mental health interventions.

## Objectives

- Enhance students’ self-awareness regarding emotions, values, and personal strengths.
- Introduce practical mindfulness and reflection techniques for everyday use.
- Equip participants with healthy coping strategies for academic and personal stress.
- Strengthen communication skills and interpersonal understanding to support peer well-being.
- Encourage the development of a positive and resilient self-image among students.

## Outcomes

- Improved ability to recognize and articulate one’s own emotions and thought patterns.
- Adoption of basic mindfulness and self-care practices for mental well-being.
- Better use of constructive coping mechanisms during stress and challenges.
- Greater confidence in supporting one’s own well-being and seeking help when needed.
