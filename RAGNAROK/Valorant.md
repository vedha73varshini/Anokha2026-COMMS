# RAGNAROK - VALORANT

VALORANT is a competitive, team-based tactical shooter that emphasizes precision, communication, and strategic planning. As part of Ragnarok, the VALORANT tournament provides a high-intensity esports experience where teams compete in structured custom matches under tournament conditions. The event challenges participants to demonstrate individual mechanical skill, effective team coordination, and adaptive in-game strategy

## Rules and Regulations

1. General Rules
  - Open only to paid and registered participants.
  - Complete registration before the deadline; on-spot subject to availability.
  - Teams may participate in multiple events if timings don't clash.
  - Report at the venue at least 15 minutes before the match.
  - Organizers' and referees' decisions are final and binding.

2. Bring Your Own PC (BYOP) Rules
  - Bring your own PC/laptop, peripherals (Mouse, Headset, Keyboard, NO MONITORS), power adapters, and cables.
  - Systems must be fully functional.
  - Power sockets and LAN access will be shared.
  - Participants are solely responsible for equipment safety and security.
  - Organizers are not responsible for damage, loss, theft, or malfunction of personal equipment.

3. Code of Conduct
  - Maintain professional and sportsmanlike behavior.
  - Harassment, abusive language, toxicity, discrimination, or unsportsmanlike conduct results in immediate disqualification.
  - Cheating, hacking, use of exploits, macros, scripts, or unauthorized third-party software is strictly prohibited.
  - Comply with all instructions from referees and event staff.

4. Game Rules & Settings
  - Make sure valorant is installed.
  - Matches on the latest stable version of Valorant.
  - Standard competitive/tournament settings will be used.
  - Maps, sides, and server settings decided by organizers
  - Report bugs or technical issues immediately to the referee.
  - Intentional exploitation of bugs/glitches results in match forfeiture or disqualification.

5. Network & LAN Rules
  - Use only the official event LAN network.
  - Do not alter network configurations or connect unauthorized devices.
  - Downloading large or non-essential files during matches is prohibited.
  - Network tampering leads to immediate removal.
  
7. Match Flow, Punctuality & Substitutions
  - Teams delaying more than 10 minutes face automatic forfeit.
  - Teams must field the minimum required number of players at match start.
  - Substitutes must be declared before the tournament begins (if allowed).
  - Pauses allowed only with referee approval.
  - Match results recorded by referees are final.
  
8. Accounts & Fair Play
  - Players must use their own personal Valorant accounts.
  - Account sharing is strictly prohibited.
  - Participants are responsible for any account bans, restrictions, or penalties.
  - Smurfing or impersonation may result in disqualification.
  
10. Streaming, Media & Publicity
  - Matches may be recorded/live-streamed by organizers.
  - Participants grant permission for use of names, photos, videos, and gameplay footage for promotional purposes.
  - Personal live streaming or recording not permitted without prior approval.
  - Stream sniping is strictly prohibited and results in immediate disqualification.

11. Disqualification & Penalties
  - Rule violations may result in warnings, match forfeiture, or disqualification.
  - Disqualified teams forfeit all prizes, rankings, and entry fees.
  - Unsportsmanlike conduct may result in a ban from future events.

12. Amendments & Organizer Rights
  - Organizers reserve the right to modify rules, format, or schedule.
  - Any changes will be communicated promptly.
  - Ignorance of the rules will not be accepted as an excuse.
